Final assessment questions:

  • Objective of the final assessment: To articulate what was learned this semester.

David Beehler:

  1. What is "software development"?
    • The process of defining requirements and writing computer programs/applications to complete the requirement.
    • Documentation of the process and code is also a part of the development process.
  2. What's the top 1 (or 2 or 3) lessons you learned from the course?
    • HTML structure, syntax and style.
    • The importance of keeping a time log.
  3. What were the stated learning objectives for the course?
    • Work as a team to understand the software development life cycle.
  4. When is software development as a group a bad idea?
    • When the group is too large.
    • If the team never gets passed the storming stage of the 4 stages of team development (forming, storming, norming and performing).
  5. Describe the software development process used for the class site.
    • SCRUM using 2 week sprint intervals.
    • Sprint retro at the end of each sprint interval.
    • Implementing code at the end of each sprint (PR to merge code into repository).
  6. Which processes that you read about most resemble what we did?
    • Agile - SCRUM
  7. Which resembled it the least?
    • Waterfall
  8. What were the goals of the development process we used?
    • Build a functioning web-site.
    • Work as a team.
  9. If you're to teach the course, how would you do it?
    • Build a backlog of the planned work (assignments) for the semester.
    • Have the students take on specific rolls during the sprint.
    • Get feedback from team (students) on how each sprint went. Successes and failures.
    • Use feedback to determine participation and grading scale.
    • Giving student's ownership (similar to how students led assignments) to help keep them engaged.

Instructor Notes

  1. "The importance of keeping a time log. ": Shows where lagging, where efficient, where needs help. Was a helpful reminder of previous habit. Also identifies distractions.
  2. "Use feedback to determine participation and grading scale.": Sometimes folks aren't as engaged on different tasks. At work, use retros to identify people who aren't pulling their weight. Don't know how to do it in classroom setting ("looks good on paper").
  3. "When is software development as a group a bad idea? ": forming means getting to know each other. Storming means working through differences. In some teams, its invisible, in some other teams, some individuals say, "my way or the highway". Never got past forming in the class since it takes time (it takes 6-8 months to "perform").
