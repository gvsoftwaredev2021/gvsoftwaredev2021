Software development is the process of coming up with, designing, programming, documenting, testing, and fixing bugs while creating and maintaining applications, and frameworks.

- When is software development as a group a bad idea?

I think that if there is a very linear project where tasks mostly have to happen one after the other then a group setting might not be the best. It could be easy to have everyone bottleneck waiting for some task before theirs to get completed before they can begin their own work. I also think that if you are developing something for fun or something you are very passionate about it could be possible that it wouldn’t lend itself to being a group project very well. There could be differing ideas on the project or different standards of work that will result in a clunky, awkward end product.

- Describe the software development process used for the class site

In class we were given assigned tasks to complete within a week long sprint. We would receive our task, work through it, ask follow up questions, and have a short retro at the end of the week. This process worked fairly well I think and allowed us time to do each task but also time for questions (usually on Wednesdays) and alterations to the task requirements if needed.

- Which processes that you read about most resemble what we did? Which resembled it the least?

I think that our process most resembled an agile and scrum methodology. It was a little bit different because we did week long sprints instead of 2 week sprints.

- What were the goals of the development process we used?

Our goals were to have a week-long sprint with a task or tasks being outlined at the beginning of each sprint. We could ask any questions as the week went on and adjust. This allowed for clear guidelines with a little bit of flexibility. Also, it allowed each of us the opportunity to lead class.
